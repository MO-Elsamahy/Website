ABM Report: Invasive Species Impact in Egypt

ABM Report: Invasive Species Impact in Egypt

Summary: Developed an Agent-Based Model (ABM) using NetLogo to simulate water hyacinth invasions in Egyptian ecosystems. Modeled interactions between invasive plants, weevils (biocontrol), fish, and algae under seasonal variations.

  • Uncontrolled hyacinths cause hypoxia and ecosystem collapse.
  • Weevils effectively reduce biomass; fish indirectly influence control efficacy.
  • Validated through micro/macro ecological patterns.
NetLogo BehaviorSpace R Mermaid

Extensions: Proposed mechanical removal, genetic adaptation, and habitat fragmentation modules.

Climate vs. Youth Unemployment in Egypt (1991–2023)

Climate vs. Youth Unemployment in Egypt (1991–2023)

Summary: Analyzed NASA temperature anomalies and World Bank unemployment data using Python statistical modeling.

  • OLS/polynomial regression, LOWESS smoothing, residual diagnostics.
  • Correlation analysis (Pearson/Spearman).
Python Jupyter pandas statsmodels scikit-learn seaborn

Findings:

  • No significant correlation (linear R²=0.034; cubic R²=0.121).
  • Post-2017 unemployment decline linked to economic reforms (IMF bailout, digital initiatives), not climate.

Policy Insight: Labor shift from agriculture (28%→20%) reduced climate sensitivity.

Determinants of Milk Production Efficiency

Determinants of Milk Production Efficiency: A Fixed Effects Panel Data Analysis of Spanish Dairy Farms

Summary: This project analyzes the efficiency of milk production across 247 Spanish dairy farms from 1993–1998. Using panel data methods, it investigates how core inputs (cows, land, labor, feed) impact milk output, tests for returns to scale, and examines input interactions. Key findings reveal cows and feed significantly drive production (elasticities: 0.66–0.38), with evidence of increasing returns to scale. Labor shows negligible effects, while land has a minor positive impact.

R plm lmtest sandwich

Methods: Fixed effects models, Translog production function, Breusch-Pagan/Hausman tests, cluster-robust SEs.

Statistical Modeling of Blood Pressure

Statistical Modeling of Blood Pressure

Summary: This study develops a regression model to predict blood pressure (BP) using predictors like age, BSA, pulse, stress, and drug utilization (DUR). After addressing multicollinearity (BSA vs. weight) and validating assumptions via log transformation, the final model identifies BSA, age, and pulse as significant predictors. The log-transformed model explains 90.1% of BP variation, with BSA having the strongest effect (21.3% increase per unit).

R car ggplot2 lmtest

Methods: Multiple linear regression, variance inflation factor (VIF), residual diagnostics, log transformation.

Time Series Analysis of the VIX

Time Series Analysis of the VIX (Volatility Index)

Summary: This project forecasts the VIX Index (1990–2025) using Box-Jenkins (ARIMA) and classical decomposition. After log transformation and annual aggregation, an AR(1) model outperforms alternatives (MAPE: 6.67%), predicting stable volatility (16–24 VIX) through 2034. The cubic trend decomposition captures nonlinear patterns but explains only 3.4% of variance, highlighting limitations in crisis prediction.

R forecast tseries seasonal

Methods: ARIMA modeling, Kruskal-Wallis test (seasonality), classical decomposition, cubic trend fitting.

E-Government Implementation in Egypt: Challenges, Impact, and Strategic Framework

Summary: This comprehensive research project analyzes Egypt's e-government ecosystem, focusing on its alignment with Egypt Vision 2030. It evaluates the current state of digital governance across healthcare, education, and public administration, identifies critical barriers (e.g., digital divide, funding gaps, cultural resistance), and measures the impact of emerging technologies (AI, IoT, blockchain) on service delivery. The study highlights Egypt's COVID-19 digital response as a catalyst for e-adoption while proposing solutions for sustainable implementation.

  • Literature Synthesis: 16+ academic studies (2014–2023), comparative analysis, systematic review.
  • Conceptual Framework: 8 critical success factors (Tech, Finance, Culture, Health, etc.), causal loop diagrams.
  • Mixed-Methodology: 400+ survey responses, expert interviews, SPSS, ISM-MICMAC modeling.
  • Case Studies: Egypt Health Passport, Banha University e-governance, blockchain for land registry.
  • Key Findings: Only 45% support fee-based e-services; Egypt ranks 103/193 in UN e-gov index; cultural resistance and funding are top barriers.
